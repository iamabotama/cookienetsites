# Locking COOK into the Liquidity Pool: What Actually Happens

**Last Updated:** June 5, 2026 (prices and pool figures as of this date)

## The Important Part, Up Front

Before the mechanics, understand exactly what is and is not guaranteed:

- **Guaranteed:** your share of the pool. Your LP receipt always entitles you to your percentage of whatever the pool contains at unlock. Nobody can take that from you, and locking does not change it.
- **Not guaranteed:** the contents or the value. The pool constantly converts between COOK and SOL as the price moves. You can come back to fewer COOK, less SOL, and fewer total dollars than you deposited, on both sides of the pair. If COOK falls, the pool spends your SOL buying COOK on the way down. If COOK rises, the pool sells your COOK on the way up, and you capture less of the rally than someone who simply held.
- **The honest framing:** the locked half is infrastructure, not an investment. The half you keep in your wallet is the investment. You give up the explosive upside on the locked portion to build the market depth that gives the unlocked portion, and everyone else's holdings, a real chance to grow at all. Today a $500 sell moves our price several percent; no serious money enters a market that thin.

Locked LP is a bet on the project, not a savings account. The fees are the income, the share is the guarantee, the value is the risk.

## The Mechanics, With Real Numbers

Say you hold 1% of COOK supply, 10M tokens, about $770 at today's price. You decide to lock half. You take 5M COOK (~$385), pair it with $385 of SOL from your wallet, deposit both into the pool, and lock the LP receipt for 12 months. The other 5M COOK stays in your wallet, untouched.

The pool constantly rebalances your locked position to stay 50/50 by value. When people buy COOK, they pull COOK out of the pool and leave SOL behind, so your COOK gradually converts to SOL as the price rises. When people sell, the reverse happens and you accumulate COOK at lower prices. You never choose when this happens. The pool does it automatically with every trade.

## Scenario: COOK Rises 10x Over the 12 Months

Roughly two thirds of your locked COOK will have been sold into the rally and converted to SOL. Your locked position would be worth about $2,400. Sounds great, except just holding the same $770 would be worth about $4,200. That gap is called impermanent loss, and it is the real cost of providing liquidity: the pool sells your winners on the way up.

## Scenario: COOK Drops 50%

Your locked position is worth about $545 and now contains more COOK than you deposited (about 7M), bought automatically on the way down with your SOL. Just holding would have been worth about $578. You lose money in both cases when price drops; nobody is protected from a falling price, in or out of the pool.

## Scenario: COOK Chops Sideways

This is the LP's best case. Price ends near where it started, impermanent loss is near zero, and you collected trading fees the whole time. At today's volume, your share of fees on a position this size is modest, on the order of $100 to $500 over the year depending on volume and the pool fee rate, and it grows if trading activity grows.

## Summary

| | Locked half (in pool) | Unlocked half (in wallet) |
|---|---|---|
| Upside if COOK runs | Partial (square root of the move) | Full |
| Downside if COOK falls | Loses value, accumulates COOK | Loses value |
| Income | Trading fees | None |
| Purpose | Market depth for the whole project | Your personal upside |

You are not donating. Your pool share belongs to you and comes back at unlock. But go in with clear eyes about what the pool does to its contents along the way.
